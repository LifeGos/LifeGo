LifeGo: Egocentric XR Lifelogging for a Digital Twin of Experience

Sehoon Kim1,2, Kunyeol Na1, Byounghyun Yoo1,3
1Intelligence and Interaction Research Center, Korea Institute of Science and Technology
2Department of AI Convergence, Soongsil University
3AI-Robotics, KIST School, Korea National University of Science and Technology
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

A life logging system that summarizes and retrieves multimodal data collected from a first-person field of view and spatially reproduces it with WebXR.

Abstract

The technology of lifelogging, which aims to record and utilize personal daily experiences and memories, has been applied in various fields such as personalized healthcare and cognitive assistance. To reduce blind spots in recording, fixed devices such as CCTV and infrared cameras are often deployed in multiple locations within a space, but their utilization is limited due to high installation costs, spatial constraints, and the burden of data storage. Similarly, wearable devices in the form of smartwatches and patches for measuring precise biometric signals are limited to distinguishing only simple actions such as walking or running due to a lack of contextual information. To overcome these limitations, this study proposes a user-perspective-centered lifelogging method that leverages web-based extended reality (WebXR) technology and eyeglass-type AR glasses. Multimodal data—including visual, auditory, and spatial information—are collected from the user’s first-person perspective, and an AI-based model processes them in real time to summarize and refine the content, preserving only essential core moments. This approach simultaneously addresses the challenges of data storage burden and privacy concerns. The proposed method provides a technical foundation for spatially recording and replaying memories within the real world, with potential applications in various fields such as memory assistance and cognitive enhancement.

Pipeline

This pipeline captures visual, spatial, and audio data from wearable devices, processes them into an egocentric context using vision, spatial, and audio analysis, and summarizes them into chronological events for easy review of daily activities.

Key Features

Multimodal Understanding

BLIP3o(이미지→텍스트) + Whisper(STT) + YaMNet(환경음) + SpatialLM(공간이해)

Event-centric Storage

요약 JSONL + 대표 프레임만 저장 → 저장량 절감

Spatial Replay

WebXR 타임라인·앵커·경로로 기억을 공간적으로 재생

Live Query

“청소기 돌렸었나?” 같은 질문에 시간대/이벤트 즉시 검색

Lightweight Web Stack

Node.js + Python + WebXR (A-Frame/Three.js)

Query Examples

💬 “어제 청소를 했었나?”

🤖 네, 어제 9시 13분부터 38분까지 총 25분동안 주로 거실 과 주방을 진공청소기를 사용하여 청소하셨습니다.

요약에서 해당 시간대 이벤트만 집계

💬 “저번 달에 자주 먹었던 음식이 뭐야?”

🤖 저번 달에는 한식을 15번, 일식을 9번, 중식을 3번 드셨습 니다. 주로 김치와 구운 고기가 포함된 한국식 가정식을 자주 즐기셨습니다.

💬 “그저께 게임 얼마나 했어?”

🤖 그저께 14시 10분부터 18시 33분까지 총 4시간 23분 동 안 컴퓨터로리그 오브 레전드(MOBA) 게임을 하셨습니다.

💬 “오늘 집에서 어떤 활동을 가장 오래 했어?”

🤖 주방에서 요리 및 설거지: 42분 거실 쇼파에서 TV 시청: 3시간 05분 컴퓨터 앞에서 업무: 2시간 13분 가장 오래 한 활동은 거실에서 TV 시청을 하신 활동입니다.
