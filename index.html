LifeGo: Egocentric XR Lifelogging for a Digital Twin of Experience

A life logging system that summarizes and retrieves multimodal data collected from a first-person field of view and spatially reproduces it with WebXR.

Abstract

The technology of lifelogging, which aims to record and utilize personal daily experiences and memories, has been applied in various fields such as personalized healthcare and cognitive assistance. To reduce blind spots in recording, fixed devices such as CCTV and infrared cameras are often deployed in multiple locations within a space, but their utilization is limited due to high installation costs, spatial constraints, and the burden of data storage. Similarly, wearable devices in the form of smartwatches and patches for measuring precise biometric signals are limited to distinguishing only simple actions such as walking or running due to a lack of contextual information. To overcome these limitations, this study proposes a user-perspective-centered lifelogging method that leverages web-based extended reality (WebXR) technology and eyeglass-type AR glasses. Multimodal dataâ€”including visual, auditory, and spatial informationâ€”are collected from the userâ€™s first-person perspective, and an AI-based model processes them in real time to summarize and refine the content, preserving only essential core moments. This approach simultaneously addresses the challenges of data storage burden and privacy concerns. The proposed method provides a technical foundation for spatially recording and replaying memories within the real world, with potential applications in various fields such as memory assistance and cognitive enhancement.

Pipeline

This pipeline captures visual, spatial, and audio data from wearable devices, processes them into an egocentric context using vision, spatial, and audio analysis, and summarizes them into chronological events for easy review of daily activities.
